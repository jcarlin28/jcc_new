## What This Is

Jessica Carlin Consulting is an independent consulting practice targeting solo practitioners and small businesses whose systems, tools, and workflows have broken down or never worked properly in the first place. It operates separately from Early Adoptr (which handles mid-size companies and startups at a higher price point) and from Atonik/So This Happened.

The positioning: systems and transformation consulting, delivered by someone who has seen this problem at every scale — from multinationals to businesses of one — and who has been a founder herself.

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Who they are
An established solo practitioner or small business owner who has been running for several years. Not a beginner — they have clients, revenue, and ambition. They have invested in tools, platforms, and people to support their growth, but built it piecemeal. The result is a system that is expensive, disconnected, and dependent on them to hold it together.

### The situation
Their tech stack doesn't talk to itself. They are paying for tools that were sold as solutions but created more problems. They have been burned — by a VA who didn't deliver, a marketing assistant who made things worse, or a platform that promised automation and delivered complexity. They have tried to fix it themselves and failed.

**Real example:** Solo wellness practitioner. Several years in business. Running Circle, Handbook, YouTube, and a lead magnet. None of the platforms connect. Lead magnet broken. Paying £5k/year for Circle and can't fix a basic issue. Has had poor experiences with marketing assistants. Systems are siloed, manual, and don't make logical sense.

### The trigger
Something small and external finally breaks — a bad customer service experience, a tool going down, a lead magnet that stops working. It is not the real problem. It is just the moment they stop tolerating the chronic one underneath it.

### What they want on the other side
- More time
- More clients
- Less anxiety — peace of mind that things run without them
- The ability to stop checking in on whether their systems are working

### Why they trust Jess over anyone else
Two things. First, she has seen this problem at every scale — from multinationals to solo founders. She pattern-matches fast and won't be condescending about a small operation or overwhelmed by the complexity. Second, she has been a founder herself. She understands what it is like to be responsible for everything with no one above you to escalate to. That is not something a specialist or a VA can credibly claim.

### Key insight on tools
Be realistic about what clients will actually use. There is no point building on a tool they are going to avoid. The question is always: ease of use and their own adoption — not what is theoretically best.

## The Offer Stack

### 1. The Clarity Call — £500 | 90 minutes

Before the call, the client completes a short intake form. Jess reviews it in advance so the 90 minutes is spent on solutions, not information gathering.

**The intake questions:**
- What tools are you currently paying for or using regularly?
- Which of those will you actually use day-to-day — and which are you avoiding? Be honest.
- What are your two or three biggest pain points right now?
- When you say your workflow isn't working, what does that actually mean? What would need to be true for things to feel like they're running properly?

The client leaves with a clear picture of what's broken and where to start. Followed by a written summary within 48 hours. No ongoing access beyond the summary.

*Total Jess time: approximately 2.5 hours including prep and write-up.*

**Right for:** Someone who knows something isn't working but isn't sure where the problem starts or ends. Often the first step into a longer engagement.

---

### 2. The Systems Audit — £3,000 | 2–3 weeks

A full diagnostic of the client's tech stack, processes, and workflows.

**Includes:**
- One 60-minute intake call
- Full review of how everything connects (or doesn't)
- Assessment of where basic automation and AI could save time — simple workflow automations, AI tools that integrate with existing setup, quick wins that don't require significant build time
- Written prioritised action plan
- One 60-minute delivery call to walk through the findings

**What is not included:** Ongoing access beyond the delivery call. Where more sophisticated AI implementation is needed (custom workflows, knowledge hub development, complex multi-tool integrations), this is scoped and quoted as a separate engagement once the audit has defined the requirement. Clients are never asked to commit to a price for work whose complexity hasn't been established first.

*Total Jess time: approximately 10–12 hours.*

**Right for:** Someone who has tried to fix things themselves and keeps hitting the same walls. Needs someone to look at the whole picture and say what actually matters.

---

### 3. Done-With-You Implementation — £1,500/month | 2–3 months

Guided implementation of what the audit surfaced.

**Includes per month:**
- 2 × 60-minute sessions
- Up to 3 hours of async support (email, document reviews, quick questions)
- Total: 5 hours per month, fixed
- Scope agreed at the start of each month

Additional hours billed at £150/hour, agreed in advance. No open-ended access. This structure exists to prevent scope creep — the EITCC situation (too many hours for not enough pay) must not be repeated.

*Total Jess time: 5 hours per month, fixed.*

**Right for:** Someone who has a clear plan but needs expert guidance to execute it without going down the wrong rabbit holes.

---

### 4. The Monthly Advisory — £750/month | minimum 3 months

A consistent thinking partner who already knows the client's business.

**Includes per month:**
- 1 × 60-minute session
- Up to 2 hours of async support
- Total: 3 hours per month, fixed

Limited to 5 clients at any one time. No open-ended email access.

*Total Jess time: 3 hours per month, fixed.*

**Right for:** Ongoing support without a big commitment. Often where clients land after implementation ends.

---

### Hours discipline — the rule across all services

Every service has a fixed hours cap. This is non-negotiable. Vague language like "async support as needed" or "available throughout the engagement" creates the conditions for unpaid overwork. Scope is agreed at the start of every engagement and every month. Anything beyond the cap is billed at £150/hour and agreed in advance.

## Brand Identity

### Visual direction
Architectural mid-century modern. Structural, precise, purposeful. The visual language feels like a systems diagram or blueprint — because that is what the work is. No filled colourful shapes. Every element has a reason to exist.

**The signature motif:** Connecting nodes — dots and lines that represent systems being mapped, understood, and fixed. Some nodes are disconnected (the problem); some are active and connected (the solution). The terracotta node is the active hub.

### Colour palette
| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Forest Green | #1B2B2A | Primary. Backgrounds, headings, the mark on light surfaces. |
| Terracotta | #C4552A | Accent only. The mark's circle, active nodes, singular emphasis. Never backgrounds. |
| Cream | #F6F5F0 | Neutral. Page backgrounds, reversed text on dark. |
| Warm Slate | #8B9E9D | Supporting. Captions, secondary text, inactive elements. |

Mustard is not part of the JCC palette.

### Typography
- **Display:** Cormorant Garamond Italic — headlines, pull quotes, wordmark
- **Body/UI:** Outfit Light/Regular/Medium — all body copy, navigation, labels

### The mark
JC monogram in Cormorant Garamond Italic, enclosed by a terracotta circle (fine stroke, not filled). Three versions: on Cream (primary), on Forest Green (reversed), on White. The terracotta circle is always present.

### Brand keywords
Direct · Precise · Practical · Grounded · Honest

## Voice & Tone

### 1. Speak to the feeling, not the symptom
The broken lead magnet is not the problem. Months of low-grade frustration with systems that don't work is the problem. Speak to what clients have been living with, not just the thing that finally snapped.

✓ *"You've known something was off for a while. Something finally broke — and now you're ready to fix all of it."*
✗ *"Is your lead magnet not converting? I can help."*

### 2. Be realistic, not reassuring
Don't tell clients what they want to hear. Tell them what they need to hear — including when a tool they love won't work for them, or when the problem is bigger than they thought. There is no point building on a tool they are not going to use.

✓ *"There's no point building on a tool you're not going to use. Let's find what actually fits how you work."*
✗ *"We can make any tool work for your business with the right setup."*

### 3. Evidence over assertion
Name the client, the problem, the outcome. The enterprise credentials build trust with a solo practitioner precisely because they show the pattern is universal — not because they are impressive in themselves.

✓ *"I've seen the same disconnected systems at Peacock and in businesses of one. The fix follows the same logic."*
✗ *"With extensive cross-sector experience, I deliver transformative results for clients of all sizes."*

### 4. Direct and boundaried
Scope is everything. Be clear about what is included and what is not — not to be difficult, but because vague engagements serve no one. Clarity upfront is a feature, not a red flag.

✓ *"Five hours per month, fixed. Scope agreed at the start of each month. No surprises."*
✗ *"I'm always available to support you as needs arise throughout our engagement."*
