06
Voice & Tone
01

Speak to the feeling, not the symptom

The lead magnet breaking is not the problem. Months of low-grade frustration with systems that don't work is the problem. Speak to what they've been living with, not just the thing that finally snapped.

"You've known something was off for a while. Something finally broke — and now you're ready to fix all of it."
"Is your lead magnet not converting? I can help."
02

Be realistic, not reassuring

Don't tell clients what they want to hear. Tell them what they need to hear — including when a tool they love won't work for them, or when the problem is bigger than they thought.

"There's no point building on a tool you're not going to use. Let's find what actually fits how you work."
"We can make any tool work for your business with the right setup."
03

Evidence over assertion

This problem exists at every scale — prove it. Name the client, the problem, the outcome. The enterprise credentials build trust with a solo practitioner precisely because it shows the pattern is universal.

"I've seen the same disconnected systems at Peacock and in businesses of one. The fix follows the same logic."
"With extensive cross-sector experience, I deliver transformative results for clients of all sizes."
04

Direct and boundaried

Scope is everything. Be clear about what's included and what isn't — not to be difficult, but because vague engagements serve no one. Clarity upfront is a feature, not a red flag.

"Five hours per month, fixed. Scope agreed at the start of each month. No surprises."
"I'm always available to support you as needs arise throughout our engagement."
