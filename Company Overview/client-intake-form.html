Before we talk,
let's get honest.

This isn't a typical intake form. These questions are designed to cut through the noise — so our first call is spent solving your actual problems, not figuring out what they are. Takes about 10 minutes. No wrong answers.

01 Who are you & what do you do?
02 What's brought you here, right now?
Something shifted — what was it? A new hire, a failed process, a leadership conversation, a deadline you missed? The "why now" tells me a lot.
Pick everything that applies.
03 Your tools — the honest version
This is the section most people get wrong. I don't need the aspirational answer. I need to know what's actually in use — and what you'll realistically stick with.
List everything — project management, comms, CRM, content, file storage, anything. Even the embarrassing stuff (spreadsheets, WhatsApp groups, shared inboxes).
When you need to find something, check something, or share something — where do you go? Be honest. It's often not the "official" tool.
If a tool requires significant time to learn and you genuinely won't invest in that — tell me. There's no point building a workflow around something that won't get used. What will you realistically adopt and maintain?
ChatGPT counts. So does "I've played with it but nothing consistent."
04 What's actually broken?
Don't tell me "our processes aren't working" — that's the symptom, not the problem. Tell me what it actually feels like: what makes you want to throw your laptop? What takes twice as long as it should? What keeps falling through the cracks?
What's been attempted, implemented, or abandoned? Knowing what didn't work is as useful as knowing what you need.
05 "Workflow" — what do you actually mean?
"Workflow" is one of those words people say to mean everything and nothing. I want to understand what it actually looks like in your world — and what kind of support would make the biggest difference.
Pick one — a campaign, a content piece, a client deliverable, a regular process. How does it start? Who's involved? Where does it get stuck? How does it end? Be specific.
What must stay? What can't change? Any constraints I should know about upfront — tools, budget, internal politics, compliance requirements?
06 What does good look like?
Not the polished vision — the real one. What would feel different? What would you be able to do that you can't do now?
This is just to help me understand what's realistic. We can talk specifics on the call.
07 Anything else?
Context, nuance, organisational dynamics, a specific ask — anything that would help me come prepared.

That's it. Thank you for taking the time to be honest — it makes everything that follows more useful.
