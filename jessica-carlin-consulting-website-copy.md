### The Clarity Call
**£500 | 90 minutes · written summary within 48 hours**

Before the call, you'll complete a short intake form covering the tools you're currently using, which ones you're actually going to use (not the ones you think you should), your biggest pain points right now, and what you actually mean when you say your workflow isn't working. Being specific here matters. There's no point building on tools you won't use or fixing problems you haven't named.

Jess reviews your answers in advance so the 90 minutes is spent on solutions, not information gathering. You leave with a clear picture of what's broken and where to start. Written summary within 48 hours.

**Right for you if:** You know something isn't working but aren't sure where the problem starts or ends. Often the first step into a longer engagement.

*~2.5 hrs total including prep and summary. Fixed scope.*

---

### The Systems Audit
**£3,000 | 2–3 weeks · intake call + written action plan + delivery call**

A full diagnostic of your tech stack, processes, and workflows. One 60-minute intake call, a thorough review of how everything connects (or doesn't), a written prioritised action plan, and one 60-minute delivery call to walk through the findings. The audit is a contained piece of work with no ongoing access beyond the delivery call, but everything you need to know what to fix and in what order.

Every audit includes an assessment of where automation and AI could save time:

- Simple workflow automations and quick wins that don't require significant setup
- AI tools that integrate with what you're already using
- Where more sophisticated implementation is needed, this is scoped and quoted separately once the audit has defined the requirement. You will never be asked to commit to a price for work whose complexity has not been established first

**Right for you if:** You've tried to fix things yourself and keep hitting the same walls. You need someone to look at the whole picture and tell you what actually matters.

*~10–12 hrs total. Fixed scope.*

---

### Done-With-You Implementation
**£1,500/month | 2–3 months · two sessions + async support per month**

Guided implementation of what the audit surfaced. Two 60-minute sessions per month plus up to 3 hours of async support: email, document reviews, quick questions. Five hours per month, fixed. Scope agreed at the start of each month so there are no surprises on either side. Additional hours billed at £150/hour, agreed in advance.

**Right for you if:** You have a clear plan but need expert guidance to execute it without going down the wrong rabbit holes.

*5 hrs/month. Fixed scope. Extra hours at £150/hr.*

---

### The Monthly Advisory
**£750/month | minimum 3 months · limited to 5 clients**

One 60-minute session per month plus up to 2 hours of async support. A consistent thinking partner who already knows the business, useful for sense-checking decisions, troubleshooting problems that come up between audits, and keeping momentum going after implementation ends.

Limited to 5 clients at any one time.

**Right for you if:** You want ongoing support without a big commitment. Often where clients land after implementation ends.

*3 hrs/month. Fixed scope. Limited availability.*

## RESULTS

**Section label:** Results

**Heading:** The same pattern across every scale

**Intro:**
Siloed tools, broken processes, systems that depend on one person to hold them together: the examples below run from a multi-national media company to a small creative team. Scale is largely irrelevant to the problem, which is why the same approach applies across the board.

**Proof points:**

→ Led a **full marketing capability assessment and operational roadmap** for a EU-government funded organisation, a 50-person EU-funded body where communications, technology, and team processes had each grown independently and never been connected.

→ Delivered a **growth marketing and creative operations audit across 5 business units** at a major news publication, with 20+ stakeholder interviews to surface what wasn't working and why.

Large organisations have the budget to commission formal audits. Small businesses have the same problems and fewer people to absorb them. The track record above is relevant to both.
