# Jessica Carlin Consulting — Website Build Brief

*May 2026*

---

## What to Build

A single-page consulting website for **Jessica Carlin Consulting**. The site has two equal jobs: convert warm inbound prospects into booked calls, and build credibility with cold prospects who've just discovered Jess.

---

## Who This Site Is For

**Established solo practitioners and small business owners** who:

- Have been running for several years — real clients, real revenue, real ambition
- Invested in tools, platforms, or people to support growth, but built it piecemeal
- Are now paying for a tech stack that's expensive, disconnected, and dependent on them to hold it together
- Have been burned before — a VA who didn't deliver, a platform that promised automation and delivered complexity
- Have tried to fix it themselves and kept hitting the same walls

**The trigger that brings them to the site:** Something small and external finally breaks — a tool goes down, a lead magnet stops working, a campaign falls apart. That's not the real problem. It's the moment they stop tolerating the chronic one underneath it.

**What they want on the other side:** More time. More clients. Less anxiety. A business that can run without them having to constantly check in.

**Why they trust Jess over anyone else:** She's seen this problem at every scale — from multinationals to solo founders — and she's been a founder herself. That combination of pattern recognition and lived experience is not something a specialist or VA can credibly claim.

**What this site is not:** This is not an Early Adoptr site. AI consulting, workshops, and podcast content sit under a separate brand. Enterprise and mid-size work also sits under that brand. This site is exclusively for solo practitioners and small businesses.

Architectural mid-century modern. Structural, precise, purposeful. No gratuitous decoration. The signature motif is **connecting nodes** — dots and lines representing systems being mapped, understood, and fixed. Some nodes disconnected (the problem state); some active and connected (the solution state). The terracotta node is the active hub. No filled colourful shapes. Every visual element has a reason to exist.

## Voice & Tone Rules

**1. Speak to the feeling, not the symptom.**
The broken lead magnet is not the problem. Months of low-grade frustration with systems that don't work is the problem. Speak to what clients have been living with, not just the thing that finally snapped.
- ✓ *"You've known something was off for a while. Something finally broke — and now you're ready to fix all of it."*
- ✗ *"Is your lead magnet not converting? I can help."*

**2. Be realistic, not reassuring.**
Don't tell clients what they want to hear. Be honest when a tool won't work for them, or when the problem is bigger than they thought.
- ✓ *"There's no point building on a tool you're not going to use. Let's find what actually fits how you work."*
- ✗ *"We can make any tool work for your business with the right setup."*

**3. Evidence over assertion.**
Name the client, the problem, the outcome. Enterprise credentials build trust with a solo practitioner precisely because they show the pattern is universal — not because they are impressive in themselves.
- ✓ *"I've seen the same disconnected systems at Peacock and in businesses of one. The fix follows the same logic."*
- ✗ *"With extensive cross-sector experience, I deliver transformative results for clients of all sizes."*

**4. Direct and boundaried.**
Be explicit about what's included and what's not. Clarity upfront is a feature, not a red flag.
- ✓ *"Five hours per month, fixed. Scope agreed at the start of each month. No surprises."*
- ✗ *"I'm always available to support you as needs arise throughout our engagement."*

### Services

**Section headline:** How I Work

**Intro paragraph:**
Every engagement is scoped to where you actually are — not where you think you should be. Most clients start with a Clarity Call, then move into an audit or ongoing support as the work develops. Whether you're a solo practitioner or running a small team, the methodology is the same: surface what's broken, prioritise what to fix, and build something that works without you having to hold it together.

---

**Service 1: The Clarity Call**
**£500 | 90 minutes**

Before the call, you'll complete a short intake form covering the tools you're currently using, which ones you're actually going to use (not the ones you think you should), your biggest pain points right now, and what you actually mean when you say your workflow isn't working. Being honest here matters — there's no point building on tools you won't use or fixing problems you haven't named.

Jess reviews your answers in advance so the 90 minutes is spent on solutions, not information gathering. You leave with a clear picture of what's broken and where to start. Followed by a written summary within 48 hours.

**The intake questions:**
- What tools are you currently paying for or using regularly?
- Which of those will you actually use day-to-day — and which are you avoiding? Be honest.
- What are your two or three biggest pain points right now?
- When you say your workflow isn't working, what does that actually mean? What would need to be true for things to feel like they're running properly?

**Right for you if:** You know something isn't working but aren't sure where the problem starts or ends. Often the first step into a longer engagement.

*Total time: approximately 2.5 hours including prep and written summary.*

---

**Service 2: The Systems Audit**
**£3,000 | 2–3 weeks**

A full diagnostic of your tech stack, processes, and workflows. One 60-minute intake call, a thorough review of how everything connects (or doesn't), a written prioritised action plan, and one 60-minute delivery call to walk through the findings. The audit is a contained piece of work — no ongoing access beyond the delivery call, but everything you need to know what to fix and in what order.

Every audit includes an assessment of where basic automation and AI could save time — simple workflow automations, AI tools that integrate with what you're already using, quick wins that don't require significant setup. Where more sophisticated AI implementation is needed (custom workflows, knowledge hub development, complex multi-tool integrations), this is scoped and quoted as a separate engagement once the audit has defined the requirement. You'll never be asked to commit to a price for work whose complexity hasn't been established first.

**Right for you if:** You've tried to fix things yourself and keep hitting the same walls. You need someone to look at the whole picture and tell you what actually matters.

*Total time: approximately 10–12 hours.*

---

**Service 3: Done-With-You Implementation**
**£1,500/month | 2–3 months**

Guided implementation of what the audit surfaced. Two 60-minute sessions per month plus up to 3 hours of async support — email, document reviews, quick questions. Five hours per month, fixed. Scope agreed at the start of each month so there are no surprises on either side. Additional hours billed at £150/hour, agreed in advance.

**Right for you if:** You have a clear plan but need expert guidance to execute it without going down the wrong rabbit holes.

*Total time: 5 hours per month, fixed.*

---

**Service 4: The Monthly Advisory**
**£750/month | minimum 3 months**

One 60-minute session per month plus up to 2 hours of async support. A consistent thinking partner who already knows your business — useful for sense-checking decisions, troubleshooting problems that come up between audits, and keeping momentum going after implementation ends.

Limited to 5 clients at any one time.

**Right for you if:** You want ongoing support without a big commitment. Often where clients land after implementation ends.

*Total time: 3 hours per month, fixed.*
